Definition

An extranet is a private network that allows controlled access to external users, such as business partners, suppliers, or customers, while still maintaining security and confidentiality. It is an extension of an intranet that enables authorized users outside the organization to access specific resources, information, or applications.

Extranets are commonly used for collaboration, sharing information, and conducting business transactions with external parties. They can provide access to shared documents, project management tools, customer portals, or supplier databases, allowing for efficient communication and collaboration while keeping sensitive information secure.

Extranets typically require authentication and authorization mechanisms to ensure that only authorized users can access the network. They may also utilize encryption and other security measures to protect data transmitted between the organization and external users.


A real-world example of an extranet is Strathmore University's Online Applications Portal which provides controlled access to applicants who wish to enroll at the university.
